<b><i>Relation to Originals</i></b>
<br>

In this Preliminaries, we provide logical facts which are inspired or implied by Newton's Principia and Euclid's Elements.
<br><br>


⦁  We present mathematical facts as they are.
<br><br>

⦁  We don't assume were or were not these facts known to Newton or Euclid. It is irrelevant to
these Preliminaies.
<br><br>

⦁  We do not do any claims these facts represent Newton's or Euclid's writings. We do not insist what Newton or Euclid said or were intended to say. We reserved the place to do such suggestions or interpretations in Addendums under original Lemma or Proposition.
<br><br>

⦁  References to original text lemma are in parenthesises.
For example,

<a target="_blank" href="?conf=sappId=addd-prel-curveXX,theorionId=proof,aspectId=curve,subessayId=areas#theorem-2">"Lemma (Theorem II)"</a>

refers to

<a target="_blank" href="?conf=sappId=b1s2prop2theor2,theorionId=proof,aspectId=english">"Book 1. Section II. Proposition II. Theorem II".</a>

 
<br><br><br><br>


<b><i>Used axiomatic frameworks</i></b>
<br>
We use two frameworks, "Euclid" and "CalculusXX".
<br><br>

<a name="Eframework"></a>
<b>"Euclid"</b> framework derives Geometrical and Ratio
properties from "Euclid's Elements".

<a target="_blank" href="?conf=sappId=addd-prel">
Real numbers apparently logically follow from E.V.5</a>
and imply
<a target="_blank" href="?conf=sappId=addd-prel#legacy-proportions">
"Legacy proportions and ratios"</a>.
<br><br>

In Newton times, complete properties of real number apparently unknown and
<a target="_blank" href="?conf=sappId=addd-prel#legacy-proportions">
"Legacy proportions and ratios"</a> have to be proven for geometrical objects or assumed
as axioms for other objects like time or force.
<br><br>

Development of Calculus and existence of ratio limits still relies on geometrical intuition.
<br><br><br>




<a name="Cframework"></a>
<b>"CalculusXX"</b> framework is Calculus which is built in XIX-XX centuries.
<br><br>

Fundamental logical sequence: Axioms of Set theory --&gt; Number theory --&gt; Rational and Real numbers --&gt; the rest, Calculus, Analytic Geometry, ... --&gt; Physics
<br><br>

The difference from "Euclid" framwork is that "Fundamental logical sequence" is more effective, Geometry is based on Number theory, and

<a target="_blank" href="?conf=sappId=addd-prel#legacy-proportions">
Legacy proportions</a>

are ignored in favour of concise Real number operations.
<br><br>
